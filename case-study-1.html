    • TuneIn Browser Application Redesign

    A self-directed UX redesign project

    Why Redesign TuneIn 📻 ?

    On some investigation, Maria and her friend learn that TuneIn aims to be the largest annotated catalog of live radio programming and on-demand radio (more than 100,000 radio stations and 4 million podcasts) from around the world with more than 75 million monthly active users as of 2019. TuneIn works on multiple platforms like voice assistants, home sound systems, multiroom entertainment platforms, Android, iOS, Windows, is even integrated with various car models, etc.

    Despite TuneIn's extensive database of diverse content, Maria identifies it's web browser application to be noticably apathetic yet the most accessible interfaces to her. Since I was looking to do a redesign piece for my portfolio, Maria asks me to try redesigning it to be more empathetic to its users. And so begins the journey of the redesign of TuneIn's web browser application.

    UX Roadmap

    In order to identify the pain points and subsequent opportunities for redesign, an iterative design process that comprised of the following UX activities were to be carried out within a self-imposed period of a month.

    1- Research

    Competitive audit

    TuneIn's business goal is steered to streaming live radio from world over on the internet on one dial and further solving for discovery.

    Since music streaming and the new "radio" (automated playlists) are impacting the market share for Live Radio, it makes it all the more crucial to observe and analyse the competitor music streaming services as well.

    Popular competitors to TuneIn as listed by web analytics service, SimilarWeb and as per an interview of the CEO of TuneIn, John Dunham by Wade Roush, a podcast producer are iHeartRadio, Sirius XM, Pandora, Deezer (which are not available in India) and Stitcher, Spotify, SoundCloud and Radio Garden among others.

    Out of these, the applications chosen for the audit were based on :

    1. Whether or not they aim to cater to a global user base.
    2. Similarity of the products they offered to TuneIn.

    User Interviews

    The user base that I convened comprised of :

    1. Obviously! Maria.
    2. Friends who use / are familiar with TuneIn or it's competitor applications.
    3. Traditional radio users who were open to the idea of engaging with a radio application on a PC.

    Five individuals from the aforementioned user base participated in an exercise to compare the competing applications across a matrix of usability and satisfaction. They were given time to familiarise themselves with the applications and then asked to plot each of the applications on the graph as seen below.

    The interview also comprised of the users discussing their real/ hypothetic motivations to use the App, how they would go about the usage and what their pain points are. We looked at a page-wise critique of the application generally comprising of its homepage, category page and the final product page.

    I also referred to user reviews from TuneIn's Facebook page and a forum called Anime Superhero which incidentally had a thread on TuneIn.

    The pain points as pointed out by the users verbatim :
    HOMEPAGE
    😕 Is this Live Radio ??
    😧 What exactly is a Podcast ?
    😒 I don't ever use the homepage.
    SEARCH, BROWSE AND NAVIGATION

    PRODUCT PAGE

    User Scenarios

    The user scenarios identified are informed by the 4 main types of user needs as discussed by Peter Morville and Louis Rosenfeld in their book, Information Architecture for the World Wide Web (3rd edition)

    1. Exploratory seeking: Shyam sets up his preferences (language, location and category) to discover relevant content for himself.
    2. Re-finding: Shyam wants to access an item he bookmarked.
    3. Known-item seeking: Maria wants to listen to music by Amy Winehouse.
    4. Exhaustive seeking: Maria wants to explore different types of content.

    Heuristic Walkthroughs with potential and existing users

    The user needs described above have been coalesced into 3 specific scenarios to undertake heuristic walkthroughs. The tasks involved in achieving the goals shall be evaluated against the widely accepted Jakob Nielson's heuristics.

    Once the violations to these heuristics are identified, they will be given a severity rating.

    • 0 - don’t agree that this is a usability problem
    • 1 - cosmetic problem
    • 2 - minor usability problem
    • 3 - major usability problem; important to fix
    • 4 - usability catastrophe; imperative to fix

    2- Making sense of it all a.k.a. Problem Definition

    "Is this Live Radio?"

    The formative research on TuneIn exposed the tough competition that Radio faces from other types of content in video and written formats on the internet. Hence, introducing potential users like Maria to "Live Radio" and "Podcasts" to navigate audio content also becomes an important function.

    For example, the homepage adorns the tagline below leading to a bad usability experience for "Exhaustive seekers" like Maria.

    Inspite of it's Unique Value Proposition being its vast catalog of Live and On-Demand Audio content, the 'common-sounding' tagline fails to communicate TuneIn's métier.

    Information Architecture (IA)

    • Taxonomy

      By shifting from its present system of organisation that appears to be a restrictive hierarchichal taxonomy, TuneIn will benefit greatly to serve it's goal of enabling discovery by adopting a combination of Facet and Hierarchichal Taxonomy.

    • Ontology

      Inspite of the company claiming local curators to be working on country specific content, on digging deep, the ontology used for regional / language specific content does not seem contextual.

    • Navigation

      Assuming a recommender system in place learning from the set preferences and historical data of the user, the navigation of the application shall comprise of :

      1. Global navigation: Home, Discover and Favourites
      2. Local navigation: Stations & Events, Podcasts & Shows
      3. Utility navigation: About, Help
      4. CTA: Log in/ Log out/Sign up, Start Free Trial, Profile
      5. Recommended product links
