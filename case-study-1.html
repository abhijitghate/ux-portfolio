    • TuneIn Browser Application Redesign

    A self-directed UX redesign project

    Why Redesign TuneIn 📻 ?

    On some investigation, Maria and her friend learn that TuneIn aims to be the largest annotated catalog of live radio programming and on-demand radio (more than 100,000 radio stations and 4 million podcasts) from around the world with more than 75 million monthly active users as of 2019. TuneIn works on multiple platforms like voice assistants, home sound systems, multiroom entertainment platforms, Android, iOS, Windows, is even integrated with various car models, etc.

    Despite TuneIn's extensive database of diverse content, Maria identifies it's web browser application to be noticably apathetic yet the most accessible interfaces to her. Since I was looking to do a redesign piece for my portfolio, Maria asks me to try redesigning it to be more empathetic to its users. And so begins the journey of the redesign of TuneIn's web browser application.

    UX Roadmap

    In order to identify the pain points and subsequent opportunities for redesign, an iterative design process that comprised of the following UX activities were to be carried out within a self-imposed period of a month.

    Research

    Competitive audit

    TuneIn's business goal is steered to streaming live radio from world over on the internet on one dial and further solving for discovery.

    Since music streaming and the new "radio" (automated playlists) are impacting the market share for Live Radio, it makes it all the more crucial to observe and analyse the competitor music streaming services as well.

    Popular competitors to TuneIn as listed by web analytics service, SimilarWeb and as per an interview of the CEO of TuneIn, John Dunham by Wade Roush, a podcast producer are iHeartRadio, Sirius XM, Pandora, Deezer (which are not available in India) and Stitcher, Spotify, SoundCloud and Radio Garden among others.

    Out of these, the applications chosen for the audit were based on :

    1. Whether or not they aim to cater to a global user base.
    2. Similarity of the products they offered to TuneIn.

    User Interviews

    The user base that I convened comprised of :

    1. Obviously! Maria.
    2. Friends who use / are familiar with TuneIn or it's competitor applications.
    3. Traditional radio users who were open to the idea of engaging with a radio application on a PC.

    Five individuals from the aforementioned user base participated in an exercise to compare the competing applications across a matrix of usability and satisfaction. They were given time to familiarise themselves with the applications and then asked to plot each of the applications on the graph as seen below.

    The interview also comprised of the users discussing their real/ hypothetic motivations to use the App, how they would go about the usage and what their pain points are. We looked at a page-wise critique of the application generally comprising of its homepage, category page and the final product page.

    I also referred to user reviews from TuneIn's Facebook page and a forum called Anime Superhero which incidentally had a thread on TuneIn.

    User Scenarios

    The user scenarios identified are informed by the 3 main types of user needs as discussed by Peter Morville and Louis Rosenfeld in their book, Information Architecture for the World Wide Web (3rd edition)

    1. Exploratory Seeking

      Maria explores different types of content. For example, Maria decides to listen to Spanish radio stations to help her with learning Spanish.

    2. Refinding

      Shyam wants to access an item he favourited.

    3. Known-item Seeking

      Shyam wants to bookmark a station he was looking for.

    Heuristic Walkthroughs with potential and existing users

    The types of user needs described above have been coalesced into 3 specific scenarios to undertake heuristic walkthroughs in order to demonstrate specific user pain points. The tasks involved in achieving the goals shall be evaluated against the widely accepted Jakob Nielson's heuristics.

    Once the violations to these heuristics are identified, they will be given a SEVERITY RATING.

    • 0 - don’t agree that this is a usability problem
    • 1 - cosmetic problem
    • 2 - minor usability problem
    • 3 - major usability problem; important to fix
    • 4 - usability catastrophe; imperative to fix

    Making sense of it all aka Problem Definition

    "Is this Live Radio?"

    The formative research on TuneIn exposed the tough competition that Radio faces from other types of content in video and written formats on the internet. Hence, introducing potential users like Maria to "Live Radio" and "Podcasts" also becomes an important function.

    For example, the homepage adorns the tagline below leading to a bad usability experience for "Exploratory seekers" like Maria.

    Inspite of it's Unique Value Proposition being its vast catalog of Live and On-Demand Audio content, the 'common-sounding' tagline fails to communicate TuneIn's métier.

    Information Architecture (IA)

    • 1. Taxonomy

      By shifting from its present system of organisation that appears to be a restrictive hierarchichal taxonomy, TuneIn will benefit greatly to serve it's goal of enabling discovery by adopting a combination of Facet and Hierarchichal Taxonomy.

    • 2. Ontology

      On digging deep, the ontology used for regional / language specific content does not seem contextual, Inspite of the company claiming local curators to be working on country specific content.

    • 3. Navigation

      The current interface for TuneIn has a strict hierarchical taxonomy under the four main categories of content- Music, Talk, Shows and Sports. Hence, options for navigation are name-sake hierarchichal menus. However, user research indicates users preferring modern modes of Discovery- from algorithms, curators, etc. Enabled by the restructured taxonomy, a recommender system learning from the set preferences and historical data of users shall facilitate a better navigation :

      1. Global Navigation : Home, Discover and Favourites
      2. Local Navigation : Stations, Events, Podcasts, Shows
      3. UTility Navigation : About, Help
      4. CTA : Log in/ Log out/Sign up, Start Free Trial, Profile
      5. Recommended Product Links

    Feature Prioritisation for the Redesign

    Feature prioritisation was estimated based on desirability and feasibility in terms of the technical overhaul.

    Ideation, User Testing and Iteration

    The affordances to be incorporated into the structure and navigation were arrived at by iterating through user flows that follow the 3 Types of User Scenarios. Feedback from 5 users and a product designer from cognitive walkthroughs with the help of low-medium fidelity prototypes were used for user testing.

    Concept Sketches

    Hand-drawn user flows were used for early discussions with fellow designers. A separate Landing Page for TuneIn was ommited in the user flow as the user research was not in support of it. Instead, an onboarding flow was voted up as an efficient way to ease into the application and populate the home page for a first time user.

    However, a concern was raised regarding the presence of certain sections in the Global navigation menus. A closed card sorting exercise was initiated. But the results of the Closed sort established the need for a Hybrid card sort due to a reported "redundant and incomplete list of categories". Following is a Dendogram analysing the Hybrid Card Sorting Exercise.

    Lo-Fi Wireframes

    Low fidelity wireframes have been used to make crucial UI decisions depending on user feedback.

    1. Utility Navigation : TuneIn categorises it's contents into 4 types: Stations, Podcasts, Events and Shows. On analysing each type for its attributes and in an attempt to optimally modularise the UI, I decided to combine the Live content- 'Stations & Events' and the On-demand content- 'Podcasts & Shows' into 2 utility navigation options. Usability tests revealed better exposure for the content as the time taken to browse reduced.
    2. Carousel : Dot navigation for the horizontal carousel was preferred.
    3. Products per view : Five stations/ podcasts per view under each category for each carousel seemed optimal.
    4. Search bar : Best practices dictate that the search bar is to be accompanied by a dedicated search button and a magnifying glass icon.
    1. White Space : White space is better managed at a macro scale to achieve good visual hierarchy for the option on the right.
    2. Filter : The option on the right incorporates a search bar within a podcast/show and the option to arrange the episodes in ascending or descending order. An icon with the drop-down menus make it more apparent and clickable.
    3. Additional Features on hover : Icons for 'share', 'like', 'comment' and playing an episode appear only on hovering over the episode as it emphasises the element in consideration.
    4. Comments section : Reviews or comments for product refinement tend to make sense. However, in this context, a negative review would have no positive impact for TuneIn possibly even affecting its brand fidelity. Therefore, the 'like' option and 'number of streams' wherever relevant was chosen as an indicator of social acceptance.

    Churning out the design

    High Fidelity Prototypes

    I used Figma to create the prototypes. The goal is to move towards a solution for all 9 of the features identified during feature prioritisation.

    EXPLORATORY SEEKING

    The prototype demonstrating the user flow for exploratory seeking serves Maria to access profile settings in order to update her preferences. It also addresses the problem specifically mentioned in Scenario-2, Heuristic Walkthrough (Amy wants to listen to music by Amy Winehouse) delivering the search results with integrity.

    RE-FINDING

    The second prototype demonstrates re-finding allowing Shyam to access a well organised favourited item. The favourited item in consideration is notified to Shyam to have a new episode out. The feature to search for a specific episode within a podcast and arrange the episodes in ascending or descending order is also incorporated.

    KNOWN-ITEM SEEKING

    The last prototype showcasing the user flow for known-item seeking demonstrates Shyam signing up to TuneIn with an option to indicate his preferences. The flow also shows Shyam searching for the station "Pravasi Bharathi" to add to his library to find later.

    Takeaways

    1. Live Radio under Threat : Although news articles released on the likes of the "World Radio Day" may argue otherwise, Radio as a medium is under threat and can only survive amongst the youth via the internet. Hence, studying the real-world context surrounding it gives us an insight into such applications' larger role. In this case, educating people about the jargon around radio, identifying its utility for specific events such as local/ national disasters and designing to incorporate such affordances can play a pivotal role in preserving it's relevance.
    2. The Experience of Search and Discovery : Although building algorithms is rightfully the developer's role, designers informed and involved with Search, Music Information Retrieval (IR) and Recommender Algorithms will prove a godsend to any team. According to my research, features such as real-time content suggestions and weighted properties like the occurence of an item being favourited, the number of streams, the date and location seemed appropriate for retrieval. A combined content-based and collaborative filtering method is the preffered choice of recommender system for its versatality.
    3. At crossroads? Look to your user! : I am swept away by the influence user/ stakeholder interactions have towards design resolution each time. These contributions must never be overlooked. That said, the opportunity to organise and conduct a successful Charrette would be a dream!
