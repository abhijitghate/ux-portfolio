    Shelter App Redesign

    System and Product Redesign
    2017-2018

    About 98 million people live in distressing sanitary conditions across urban slums in India. The lack of household toilets pose not just health risks but are also safety risks to women and other vulnerable demographics like the disabled, the elderly or the ill that access community toilets or defecate in the open away from their homes, especially post sundown. The problem of sanitation in urban slums have plagued generations of urban planners. There is no universal model that works across cultures to solve this problem. Here we introduce you to one that seems to be gaining momentum in the state of Maharashtra through years of patient research, experimentation and evolution.

    Introducing the OHOT Model

    Recognizing the problems posed by open defecation as well as community toilets that have their own set of disadvantages; Shelter Associates (SA), a Pune based grassroots level non-profit has over the years perfected a safe and affordable solution to the sanitation problem that focuses on facilitating individual household toilets through its data driven, replicable, scalable and multi-stakeholder inclusive ‚ÄòOne Home One Toilet‚Äô (OHOT) model . The Initiative was awarded The Local Project Challenge Honor Award 2020 at The World Urban Forum by the UN Habitat.

    The model involves the construction materials facilitated by our donors being delivered at the beneficiaries' doorstep free of cost for them and the beneficiary family contributing towards the construction of the toilet instilling a sense of ownership among the beneficiaries.

    My Role

    My engagement with SA was piqued by my interest in mapping informal human settlements for their documentation and progression. I worked in the capacity of a designer redesigning the product called Shelter App and in the process also worked on some very interesting and niche spatial data management and visualisation.

    Problems identified

    On conducting usability tests of the existing user flows with the Executive Director, M&E Manager, Project Manager and GIS analysts, these broad problems were identified:

    1. Due to different individuals handling data collection and other socio-political changes in the scenario being surveyed, 20 years' worth of data on sanitation of informal settlements across these 5 districts was in different formats, hence, unfit for integration and analysis.
    2. Dashboards designed were not optimised to be used by different stakeholders.
    3. Using a proprietary GIS software which hasn't had any updates in the past 5 years was affecting the team's scalability and efficiency.
    4. Maps made by different analysts followed different cartographic styles due to the lack of a cartographic style guide.

    Red Routes

    Based on the problems identified and requirements collected from the users, the following red routes were proposed:

    1. Survey form redesign and Data migration
    2. Dashboard redesign

    1. Survey form redesign and Data migration

    We switched to an open-source web-based application called Kobo Toolbox for defining and deploying survey forms and it's android app which also works offline for data collection. Surveys were redesigned to incorporate form logic that includes validating and restricting responses and types, conditional questions, setting defaults and cascading lists.

    The data collection was segregated into the following surveys:

    1. Rapid Infrastructure Mapping ‚Äì mapping of existing infrastructure of slum
    2. Rapid Household Surveys ‚Äì collecting household level data
    3. Individual factsheet ‚Äì for maintaining record of completed toilets

    The process involved charting elaborate decision trees on MindMeister for each of the surveys and defining them on Kobo Form Builder as per the logic developed with user research.

    All the existing data in multiple formats was migrated to the newly created forms. This migrated data then fed into the dashboards designed for different users.

    2. Dashboard redesign

    The visual language chosen for the project was Material Design.

    The Executive Director and the M&E Manager had similar emerged that required different dashboards or interfaces within the Shelter App.

    The prime real estate on the dashboard is dedicated to highlighting deviations in operational data. All other analytical KPIs that drive targeting are shown below the fold.

    The architecture of the app consisted of a homepage with all non-private data on sanitation in slums collected by Shelter displayed to any user that lands on the application. This page would allow users with a login ie., the users identified as the user personas, to login and access their respective dashboards.

    Goals

    1. The dashboard for each user group would serve as a form of access for all the data that Shelter collects.
    2. It must allow the user to zero-in on specific data points and further inspect it for deeper insights.
    3. It must answer frequently asked questions at a glance.

    The dashboard was designed to highlight operational data over analytics. The users also demonstarted the need for the dashboard to be used for presentations. Hence certain KPIs for each user groups were also identified and incorporated into the later parts of the dashboard.
